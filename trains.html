Train Symbols

Trains! WSOR's current train symbols have not been around for relatively long, but they are quite easy to memorize and make some actual sense unlike some Class I railroads.

The first and only letter in a WSOR train symbol is either "T" for road trains, "L" for local trains, or "Y" for yard movement trains. As for the number, road trains are pretty straightforward as they just go from 1-10. Local trains, however, have three digit numbers in which the first digit represents the city the train is based out of. 2 is Milwaukee, 3 is Janesville, 4 is Madison, 5 is Horicon, and 8 is Baraboo. The last two digits are seemingly picked at random. The same numbering system can be applied with yard trains, with the exception of the last two digits simply being 01 or 02 instead.
