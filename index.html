The Wisconsin and Southern Railroad Unofficial Cheatsheet

T4! WAMX 6022! L595! Watertown Sub! What the heck do these even mean? And how does everything fit together?

The 837 mile-long, Watco-owned, Class II Railroad none other than the Wisconsin & Southern has garnished an impressively large following in the realm of railroad enthusiasts, photographers, and videographers.

Founded in 1980 from the ashes of the recently bankrupt Milwaukee Road, the railroad quickly grew and later acquired some additional lines that belonged to the Chicago & North Western. With the infamous red and white paint scheme coupled with Milwaukee Road heritage right on the nose, the "goose" has become more and more treasured by fans of the rail in Wisconsin, Illinois, and elsewhere.

There exist multiple online communities dedicated to discussing the WSOR and also serve to track the whereabouts of its many trains. Understandably so, people often get confused about its operations and how they all work together, as operations can change at any time and are not always consistent due to the smaller nature of the railroad.

Because of this, this handy website was created in order to inform people about the many aspects of the WSOR with the goal of providing people with accurate information to make catching (and chasing) its trains much easier.

If you have any questions about operations or have any new or updated information, please click "CONTACT" at the top or bottom which will take you to a Google Form.

Thank you all so much and stay safe on any adventures!

Useful External Resources

Watco official site

The official site for WSOR from Watco has some basic information about its operations, as well as some interesting static and interactive route maps.

Line operating information by Chicago Transit & Railfan

Bill Vandervoort's extensive site, Chicago Transit & Railfan, has loads of information regarding past and present Midwest railroads and their many lines and operating information.

Under the Wisconsin short line railroads page, There are 17 lines listed under Wisconsin & Southern, each with their own link. Each line has information about its operation history and a breakdown of its mileposts, and can sometimes include track diagrams, lost stations, and interlocking towers.

Wisconsin Railroads and Harbors 2023 Map

Every year, the Wisconsin DOT releases a new version of their official map of all railroads and harbors in the state. This can be useful to see how WSOR lines interact and interchange with other railroads.

Official railroad map of Wisconsin, 1900

All the way back in the year 1900, an extensive map was created by the Railroad Commission of Wisconsin which detailed every single rail line in the state of Wisconsin. This map has since been preserved by the Library of Congress and is available to be viewed in high quality.
